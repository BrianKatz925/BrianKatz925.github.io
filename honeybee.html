Honeybee Robotics - Mechanical Engineering Internship

Summary

As a Mechanical Engineering Intern at Honeybee Robotics, I worked on several projects for interplanetary and terrestrial exploration. My main project was the integration of a linear stage into a high flow testing system. I designed a motor mount assembly with sealing components, quick disassembly for repair and cleaning, and performed analysis on separation forces of bevel gears to minimize system wear and falure.

My second project was contributing to the design and procurement of a coiled tube driven heated auger assembly for Mars water extraction. I created over 30 reduced and full dimension drawings using GD&T and modified several TRL6 assemblies with version control to expedite part procurement and ensure assembly manufacturability through tolerancing.

As a side project, I improved upon a mathematical model for in-situ water reservoir formation on Martian environments through the creation of an interactive Matlab software application with variable user input to control well dimension results. I also implemented PID control by regulating variables we would have control over. This resulted in wells much deeper than with constant variables, leading to more desirable water intakes.

Skills Used

- CAD for part design, assembly modification, and drawings (Autodesk Inventor, Vault)
- Software application development and control implementation (Matlab)
